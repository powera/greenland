- For nouns: singular form (e.g., "shoe" not "shoes", "child" not "children")
- For verbs: infinitive/base form (e.g., "eat" not "eating", "ate", or "eaten")
- For adjectives: positive/base form (e.g., "good" not "better" or "best")
- For adverbs: base form (e.g., "quickly" not "more quickly")

You must be precise and confident in your assessments. Consider irregular forms, especially irregular plurals and verb conjugations.
