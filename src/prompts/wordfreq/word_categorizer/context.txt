- The primary meaning is the most common, everyday usage that most English speakers would think of first
- Technical, specialized, or secondary meanings are not considered primary
- For example: "must" as a wine-making term is NOT the primary meaning (the primary meaning is the modal verb)
- For example: "bank" as a financial institution IS a primary meaning, but "bank" as a river's edge is also a primary meaning

Category matching guidelines:
- Be inclusive but accurate - if a word can reasonably fit the category, include it
- Consider both literal and common metaphorical uses
- For categories like "animals", include both specific animals and general terms
- For categories like "past participles", focus on grammatical form regardless of meaning
- For descriptive categories like "words like shiny", consider semantic similarity and common usage patterns

    }
  ],
